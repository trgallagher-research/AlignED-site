Why This Matters

The case for evaluating AI alignment with educational research

The Challenge

AI systems are increasingly deployed in educational settings—as tutors, teaching assistants, and content generators. Yet these systems may reinforce educational misconceptions that persist despite decades of research debunking them.

Consider neuromyths: false beliefs about the brain and learning that remain widespread among educators. Research consistently shows that around 50% of teachers believe claims like "we only use 10% of our brains" or "students learn best when taught in their preferred learning style." When AI systems perpetuate these misconceptions, they risk entrenching them further.

What Makes AlignED Different

Standard AI benchmarks test academic content—mathematics, coding, general knowledge. AlignED tests something different: alignment with evidence-based educational research.

We focus on what teachers need to know, not what students should learn:

  • Misconception rejection: Can the model identify and reject neuromyths with appropriate confidence?
  • Diagnostic reasoning: When evidence-based strategies fail in practice, can the model explain why?
  • Pedagogical knowledge: Does the model demonstrate cross-domain teaching expertise?

Beyond Accuracy

A model that scores 90% on a benchmark under ideal conditions may perform very differently in practice. AlignED examines multiple dimensions:

Temperature Robustness

How stable is performance across different temperature settings? A model that only performs well at T=0 may be brittle.

Prompt Sensitivity

Does rephrasing questions change answers? We test standard, interrogative, embedded, and adversarial framings.

Confidence Calibration

Are models appropriately uncertain when they should be? Overconfidence in wrong answers is particularly problematic in education.

Token Efficiency

How much reasoning does the model require? Efficiency matters for real-world deployment.

Grounded in Research

AlignED benchmarks are adapted from peer-reviewed instruments and validated assessments:

  • Neuromyths Survey: Adapted from Dekker et al. (2012), the foundational study documenting neuromyth prevalence among teachers
  • Implementation Scenarios: Developed from research on why evidence-based practices fail in real classrooms
  • Pedagogical Knowledge: Based on validated teacher certification assessments

Who This Is For

Policymakers and Governance Bodies

AI governance requires transparent evaluation. AlignED provides standardised benchmarks that can inform deployment decisions, procurement guidelines, and regulatory frameworks.

Educational Researchers

AlignED offers a framework for evaluating AI educational capabilities with open methodology and reproducible results. The platform supports both model comparison and longitudinal tracking.

AI Developers

AlignED tests capabilities that standard benchmarks miss. Understanding where models succeed or fail on educational knowledge can guide training and development priorities.

What We Can Claim

AlignED tests how well AI responses align with evidence-based educational research. Our benchmarks adapt peer-reviewed instruments for AI evaluation.

What we do not claim: Benchmark performance does not prove "understanding" or predict real-world tutoring effectiveness. High scores indicate alignment with research evidence, not certification for deployment.

The Educational Alignment Index

The Educational Alignment Index (EAI) is our composite score, combining performance across all benchmarks and evaluation dimensions. It provides a single metric for comparing models while maintaining transparency about component scores.
