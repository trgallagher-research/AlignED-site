AlignED

Transparent benchmarks for AI in education

Evaluating AI alignment with educational research evidence

AI systems are increasingly used to support teaching and learning. AlignED tests whether these systems align with evidence-based educational research—from rejecting common misconceptions to diagnosing why proven strategies sometimes fail in practice.

Explore Methodology View Results

Why This Matters

AI in education needs transparent, evidence-based evaluation

The Problem

AI systems can reinforce educational misconceptions. Standard benchmarks test academic content knowledge, not pedagogical reasoning or alignment with research evidence.

Our Approach

AlignED tests what teachers need to know: rejecting neuromyths, understanding why evidence-based strategies succeed or fail, and demonstrating pedagogical knowledge.

Beyond Accuracy

We examine how performance changes across conditions—temperature settings, prompt variations, confidence levels—revealing whether responses reflect robust knowledge.

Current Benchmarks

Evaluation instruments adapted from peer-reviewed research

Neuromyths Survey

32 items testing rejection of brain-based misconceptions, adapted from Dekker et al. (2012). Human teachers average around 50% accuracy.

Implementation Scenarios

12 diagnostic scenarios testing whether models can identify why evidence-based strategies fail in specific classroom contexts.

Pedagogical Knowledge (CDPK)

920 items from validated teacher certification assessments, testing cross-domain pedagogical knowledge.

Special Education (SEND)

223 items assessing knowledge of special educational needs and disability, essential for inclusive teaching.

For Policymakers

AI systems are increasingly used in educational contexts. AlignED provides transparent, standardised benchmarks to evaluate whether these systems align with educational research evidence—helping inform decisions about responsible deployment.

Our methodology is open, our data is accessible, and our results are designed to support governance and policy decisions.
