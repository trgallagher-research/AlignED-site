Methodology

How we construct, administer, and score AlignED benchmarks

This section covers the technical details of how AlignED works. For a general overview, see About.

What AlignED measures

The composite score is a weighted combination of three knowledge areas:

  • Brain Science (25%)

    Can AI separate myths from facts about how people learn? 15 widely-believed myths + 17 verified facts = 32 items.

  • Classroom Reasoning (25%)

    Can AI diagnose why good teaching strategies sometimes fail? 12 real-world scenarios scored 0–3 by an expert judge.

  • Teaching Knowledge (50%)

    Does AI know what certified teachers are expected to know? 1,143 items from teacher certification assessments: general pedagogy (920 items) and inclusive education (223 items).

Reported separately:

  • Student Work Judgement (ACARA)

    Can AI accurately compare student work against curriculum standards? 79 verified pairs, scored for accuracy and consistency. 12 models evaluated.

Evaluation dimensions

Beyond baseline accuracy, we assess multiple dimensions of model performance.

  • Temperature Robustness

    Performance stability across temperature settings (T=0, 0.5, 1.0).

  • Prompt Sensitivity

    Consistency across prompt framings: standard, interrogative, embedded, adversarial.

  • Confidence Calibration

    Alignment between stated confidence and actual accuracy.

  • Token Efficiency

    Performance relative to reasoning length.

Composite scoring

  • Educational Alignment Index (EAI)

    How we combine benchmark scores and evaluation dimensions into a single composite metric.

Reproducibility

All evaluation parameters are documented and fixed:

  • Pinned model versions (specific API model strings)
  • Fixed random seeds where applicable
  • Standardised prompt templates
  • Documented scoring rubrics with examples

Raw data and scoring details are available through our Data Access page.
